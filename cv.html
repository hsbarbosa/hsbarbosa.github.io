Curriculum Vitae

Dr Hugo Barbosa is Senior Lecturer in Human Dynamics and Urban Systems at the University of Exeter, working at the intersection of data science, network science, and urban mobility.

Download PDF Google Scholar ORCID

Profile

My research focuses on how people move through cities and how mobility systems shape inequality, opportunity, and urban wellbeing. I develop computational methods using large-scale behavioural data to generate interpretable evidence for transport and planning policy.

I collaborate across computer science, urban studies, and applied network science, with work published in leading interdisciplinary journals.

Academic Background

  • PhD in Computer Science, Florida Institute of Technology (2016).
  • MSc in Computer Engineering (Artificial Intelligence), Polytechnic School of Pernambuco (2011).
  • BSc in Computer Engineering, Polytechnic School of Pernambuco (2009).

Appointments

  • Senior Lecturer in Human Dynamics and Urban Systems, University of Exeter (2025-present).
  • Lecturer in Human Dynamics and Urban Systems, University of Exeter (2019-2025).
  • Postdoctoral Researcher, University of Rochester (2016-2019).

Research Areas

  • Human mobility and activity patterns across scales.
  • Urban systems, transport equity, and spatial inequality.
  • Network science and data-driven modelling for policy-relevant insights.
