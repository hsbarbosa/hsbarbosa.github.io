Research

Questions about movement, cities, and inequality approached computationally.

My research focuses on how people move through cities and how mobility systems structure opportunity, segregation, and resilience. The work blends network science, large-scale behavioural data, and interpretable modelling.

Methods

  • Network science
  • Computational social science
  • Large-scale data analysis
  • Statistical modelling

Theme 01

Human mobility

Quantitative analysis of mobility patterns across spatial and temporal scales, with an emphasis on explanatory models and policy-relevant interpretation.

Theme 02

Urban systems

Data-driven understanding of urban structure and dynamics, including links between movement, inequality, accessibility, and livability.

Theme 03

Methods

Computational and statistical approaches to complex systems, including reproducible data workflows and model-based reasoning over behavioural data.
